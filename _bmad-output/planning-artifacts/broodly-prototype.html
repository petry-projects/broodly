Notification Types

Learn what each notification type looks like and control which ones you receive.

⚠️ Seasonal Risk Alerts
Triggered when seasonal conditions create swarm risk, starvation risk, pest pressure, or queen issues.
EXAMPLE
⚠ Swarm risk elevated — Hive 3
Warm temps + strong nectar flow. Consider inspecting for queen cells this week.
⚖️ Telemetry Alerts
Triggered by sudden weight changes, temperature anomalies, or sustained trends from connected sensors.
EXAMPLE
🚨 Sudden weight drop — Hive 4
Lost 4.2 lbs in 2 hours. Possible swarm event. Check immediately.
🔍 Inspection Reminders
Reminds you when inspections are overdue or upcoming based on your schedule and seasonal timing.
EXAMPLE
📅 Inspection due — Backyard Apiary
Last inspected 10 days ago. Spring buildup phase recommends 7-day intervals.
📋 Task & Follow-Up Reminders
Notifies you about pending actions, unresolved tasks, and escalation of high-priority items after 48 hours.
EXAMPLE
⏰ Follow-up overdue — Hive 3 queen cell check
Scheduled for Mar 28. Re-inspect to verify queen cell removal was successful.
🌟 Skill Milestones
Celebrates your progress as you complete inspections and build beekeeping skills.
EXAMPLE
🎉 10 inspections complete!
Your guidance will now include more concise options. Nice work!
💬 Feedback Prompts
Periodic quick surveys (max 1 every 2 weeks) to measure how confident you feel about your decisions.
EXAMPLE
📊 How confident did you feel?
Rate your confidence in today's inspection decisions (1-5). Optional free-text feedback.

Safety First

Newbie

Before you open the hive

Confirm your safety gear and conditions.

✓ Wearing full bee suit with veil secured
✓ Gloves on, no exposed skin
✓ Smoker lit and producing cool white smoke
Hive tool and frame grip ready
🧤 Tip: Light your smoker 5 minutes before opening. Use pine needles or burlap for fuel.

Shown for Newbie beekeepers only. Amateur and Sideliner skip to inspection.
