# Product Brief: bmad-method

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Executive Summary

`bmad-method` equips beekeepers for success by turning uncertainty into confident, timely action that improves hive health and survivability. Backyard beekeeping is growing rapidly, yet many new beekeepers face preventable losses because they lack clear, contextual guidance at decision moments. More experienced beekeepers face a different challenge: operational complexity that reduces efficiency and consistency. This product serves both groups with practical, skill-adaptive recommendations focused on what to do next and why it matters. Success is defined by measurable behavior change (better timing and decision quality) and measurable outcomes (higher survival, healthier hives, stronger productivity).

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Beekeepers, especially Newbies, struggle to make confident, context-aware decisions during critical hive moments, leading to preventable losses, delayed interventions, and inconsistent results.

### Problem Impact

- Newbie beekeepers lose colonies and confidence due to uncertainty and avoidable mistakes.
- Experienced beekeepers lose efficiency from fragmented workflows and unclear action priorities.
- Preventable losses increase cost, reduce productivity, and slow beekeeper progression.
- The ecosystem loses resilience when avoidable hive-health decline remains common.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- Guidance is often generic rather than localized, seasonal, and situation-specific.
- Many tools present data but don’t convert it into clear, prioritized next actions.
- Advice is rarely adapted to user skill level, causing overload for beginners and friction for advanced users.
- Limited transparency around recommendation rationale reduces trust and follow-through.

### Proposed Solution

A beekeeping success platform that provides clear, context-aware, skill-adaptive guidance so users can confidently take the right action at the right time. It combines planning, in-field decision support, and explainable recommendations to reduce preventable losses for Newbies while improving efficiency and consistency for experienced beekeepers.

### Key Differentiators

- Confidence-first design for Newbies at high-risk decision points.
- Action-priority engine focused on “what to do next” instead of passive data display.
- Skill-adaptive guidance depth across beginner, intermediate, and advanced beekeepers.
- Explainable recommendations that build trust and improve follow-through.
- Outcome-led model targeting measurable gains in hive health, survival, and productivity.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

1) Newbie Beekeepers (Primary Priority)

- Persona: Hannah, 34, first-year backyard beekeeper with 2 hives, balancing a full-time job and family life.
- Context and goals: Wants healthy hives, confidence during inspections, and to avoid preventable losses.
- Problem experience: Feels unsure interpreting hive signals, second-guesses feeding/treatment timing, and relies on scattered advice.
- Current workarounds: Paper notes, generic reminders, and ad-hoc online guidance.
- Success vision: “I know exactly what to do this week, why it matters, and my hives are getting healthier.”

2) Amateur Beekeepers

- Persona: Marcus, 42, 2-5 years experience, 8-20 hives across one or two sites.
- Context and goals: Improve outcomes and consistency while managing limited time.
- Problem experience: Juggles many tasks without clear prioritization and misses high-impact checks in busy weeks.
- Current workarounds: Spreadsheet tracking, manual reminders, and memory-based planning.
- Success vision: “I can prioritize the right tasks quickly and improve survival/productivity without extra overhead.”

3) Sideliner Beekeepers

- Persona: Elena, 51, experienced sideliner with 50+ hives across multiple locations.
- Context and goals: Maximize operational efficiency and reduce missed opportunities/losses.
- Problem experience: Complex scheduling, travel overhead, and repeated decision burden across many hives.
- Current workarounds: Custom logs, fragmented tools, and route-by-habit decision making.
- Success vision: “I run a tighter operation with fewer costly misses and better output per hour.”

### Secondary Users

- Mentor: Influences newbie trust and guidance adoption; benefits from read-only visibility and coaching context.
- Partner: Influences household/business adoption; supports planning and execution without being the primary operator.
- Inspector: Influences compliance expectations; benefits from structured records and action traceability.

### User Journey

- Discovery: Finds the product through beekeeping communities, mentors, or search while trying to avoid losses.
- Onboarding: Enters location, hive setup, goals, and experience level; receives confidence-first setup.

- Emotional arc: anxious → guided → confident.

Amateur Journey

- Discovery: Seeks better planning and prioritization across multiple hives.
- Onboarding: Imports or enters existing hive and management context.
- Core usage: Uses a decision queue and schedule guidance to focus on highest-impact work.

- Long-term: Relies on the platform as the primary planning and decision-support workflow.
- Emotional arc: overloaded → organized → in control.

Sideliner Journey

- Discovery: Looks for operational leverage and reduced labor waste.
- Onboarding: Configures multi-location operation profile and management goals.
- Core usage: Executes prioritized plans, logs field actions quickly, and reviews operation-level risk and success indicators.

- Long-term: Uses the platform as an efficiency and outcome optimization layer for the operation.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

- Live Inspection Co-Pilot (Must-have): Real-time, step-by-step inspection guidance that helps users decide what to do next during hive checks.
- Personal Baseline Advisor (Must-have): Recommendation layer that compares current hive conditions to user-specific historical patterns and local context to improve decision confidence.

### Out of Scope for MVP

- Retailer integration: No vendor/retailer shopping integration in MVP; deferred to a future release.

### MVP Success Criteria

- Primary success gate: MVP passes live field testing with acceptance testers.
- Validation intent: Testers can complete real inspections using the product and report that guidance is actionable, confidence-improving, and practical in field conditions.

### Future Vision

- Swarm Risk Predictor: Predictive alerting for likely swarm risk before costly outcomes occur.
- Adaptive Learning Path: Skill-aware coaching that evolves with user capability over time.
- 911 mode: Emergency-support capability for critical incidents (subject to safety/legal validation).
- Conversational Inspection Companion: A voice-driven Q&A capability where beekeepers can ask natural-language questions during inspections and receive context-aware, skill-adapted answers grounded in their hive history, regional norms, and domain knowledge.

## Monetization Framework

### Tier Structure

- **Free Tier (Permanent):** Single apiary (up to 3 hives), basic guided inspections, seasonal calendar, limited recommendation history (30 days). Purpose: acquisition funnel, habit formation, and word-of-mouth driver.
- **Beekeeper Tier (~$5-8/month or ~$50-70/year):** Unlimited hives and apiaries, full recommendation history, Vision AI analysis, voice logging, data export, read-only collaborator access. Target: serious hobbyists and amateurs.
- **Pro Tier (~$12-18/month or ~$120-160/year):** Everything in Beekeeper plus multi-location operational dashboard, batch operations, advanced predictive alerts, compliance-formatted exports, priority support. Target: sideliners and small commercial operators.

### Pricing Strategy Principles

- Anchor free tier to demonstrate recommendation value before paywall.
- Gate operational scale features (multi-location, batch), not core guidance quality.
- Price below the cost of a single lost colony (~$150-250 replacement cost) to make ROI argument self-evident.
- Offer annual discount (2 months free) to improve LTV and reduce churn.

### Freemium Boundary Rules

- Never gate safety-critical recommendations behind payment.
- Free tier must be genuinely useful for a first-year beekeeper with 1-3 hives.
- Upgrade triggers should align with natural scaling moments (adding hives, second season, adding apiaries).

## Community and Network Strategy

- **Regional Knowledge Network:** Anonymized, aggregated signals from users in the same climate zone create "what's happening in your area" context cards (e.g., "72% of beekeepers in your region have started spring feeding this week"). This transforms individual usage into collective intelligence without requiring explicit social features.
- **Mentor Matching:** Connect experienced beekeepers willing to mentor with newbies in the same region. Mentors get a dashboard showing their mentee's hive status (with permission), enabling targeted advice. This creates acquisition loops (mentors invite mentees) and retention loops (mentees stay because of the relationship).
- **Association Integration:** Partner with state/local beekeeping associations to offer the app as a member benefit. Associations get anonymized aggregate data about member colony health trends.

## Data Asset Strategy

The product's longitudinal dataset — linking management actions to colony outcomes across diverse regions, climates, and skill levels — will become an increasingly valuable and defensible asset over time.

- **Outcome-Calibrated Recommendations:** As data accumulates, recommendations improve because outcome feedback tunes the model. This creates a flywheel: more users generate more outcome data, which improves recommendations, which attracts more users.
- **Regional Baselines:** Aggregated anonymized data produces the most accurate regional seasonal baselines in the industry. These baselines become the foundation for recommendation accuracy and are impossible to replicate without distributed user presence.
- **Research Partnership Value:** Anonymized, structured colony-health datasets are valuable to university researchers, USDA programs, and pollinator health initiatives. Offering research-grade data access (with user consent) creates institutional partnerships, brand credibility, and potential co-funding opportunities.
- **Benchmarking Service:** Enable beekeepers to compare their colony outcomes against anonymized regional averages — a retention driver and a premium feature candidate.

## Growth Loops and Viral Mechanics

### Natural Sharing Triggers
- **Inspection Summary Sharing:** After completing an inspection, offer a one-tap "Share with my mentor" or "Share with my bee club" export that produces a clean, branded summary card.
- **Seasonal Achievement Cards:** Generate shareable visual cards for seasonal milestones ("My colonies survived winter!" or "First honey harvest: 42 lbs"). Each card includes subtle product attribution.

### Referral Mechanics
- **Mentor Referral Loop:** When a user adds a mentor collaborator, the mentor receives an invitation that highlights the dashboard they will access. One active mentor can drive 5-15 new user acquisitions per season.
- **Bee Club Group Onboarding:** Offer a "Start your club on Broodly" flow where a club leader creates a group and invites members.

### Content-Driven Acquisition
- **Public Seasonal Guidance Pages:** Publish region-specific, seasonally updated "What to do this week" web pages derived from the recommendation engine. These rank for high-intent search queries and serve as top-of-funnel acquisition.
